This website examines how a modern video game represents historical warfare and what that reveals about technology, society, and media.

Thesis

Call of Duty: World at War portrays World War II combat with strong visual realism, but simplifies the technological and ethical consequences of industrialized warfare. While immersive, it transforms mass destruction and human suffering into entertainment, raising important questions about how technology—particularly military innovation—shapes both war and our perception of it.

Overview

This project analyzes how the game represents World War II technology, evaluates its accuracy, and explores its broader social and ethical implications. By connecting the game to key course concepts such as technological determinism and the ethics of technology, this website demonstrates how technological systems influence both warfare and society.

Plot Summary

Call of Duty: World at War is set during World War II and follows soldiers fighting in both the Pacific and Eastern Front campaigns. The game presents intense battles against enemy forces, emphasizing the brutality and chaos of war. Players experience the conflict through multiple perspectives, highlighting both individual survival and large-scale military operations.
