Call of Duty: World at War (2008)

Technology and Society Final Project

By Yasir Shirazi

Home Technology Accuracy Social Impact Course Connections Entertainment Cast & Devs

Thesis

Call of Duty: World at War portrays World War II combat with visual realism, but simplifies the technological and ethical consequences of industrialized warfare. While immersive, it transforms mass destruction and human suffering into entertainment, raising important questions about how technology shapes both war and our perception of it.

Overview

This project analyzes how the game represents World War II technology, evaluates its accuracy, and explores its broader social and ethical implications. By connecting the game to real-world historical developments and course concepts, this website demonstrates how technology influences both warfare and society.

Plot Summary

Call of Duty: World at War is set during World War II and follows soldiers fighting in both the Pacific and Eastern Front campaigns. The game presents intense battles against enemy forces, emphasizing the brutality and chaos of war. Players experience the conflict through multiple perspectives, highlighting both individual survival and large-scale military operations.
