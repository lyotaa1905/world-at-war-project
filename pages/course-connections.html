Technological Determinism

Technological determinism is the idea that technology shapes how societies develop and function. As discussed in Lecture [X], advancements in military technology during World War II significantly influenced how wars were fought. Call of Duty: World at War reflects this concept by emphasizing how improved firearms, explosives, and combat strategies shape the gameplay experience. However, the game simplifies this concept by focusing on action while reducing the complexity of real-world warfare.

Military-Industrial Complex

The military-industrial complex refers to the relationship between governments, military forces, and industries that produce weapons and technology. As covered in Lecture [X] and Chapter [X, p. XX], World War II relied heavily on large-scale industrial production. World at War reflects this through its portrayal of standardized weapons and coordinated military operations, demonstrating how warfare is shaped by both technology and economic systems.

Ethics of Technology

The ethics of technology examines whether technological advancements should be used simply because they are possible. Weapons such as flamethrowers and explosives raise significant ethical concerns due to their destructive nature. As discussed in class, technological innovation often creates moral dilemmas, especially in warfare. In World at War, these technologies are presented as gameplay tools, which can reduce awareness of their real-world consequences and human impact.

Overall Connection to the Course

These concepts demonstrate how technology influences both historical events and modern interpretations of those events. World at War provides a simplified representation of complex realities, allowing players to engage with key ideas from the course while also highlighting the limitations of media in accurately portraying warfare and its consequences.
