Technological Determinism

Technological determinism is the idea that technology shapes how societies develop and function. In World War II, advancements in weapons and military equipment significantly influenced how wars were fought. Call of Duty: World at War reflects this concept by showing how improved firearms, explosives, and combat strategies changed the nature of warfare. As discussed in class lectures, technological innovation often drives historical change, and this is clearly demonstrated through the evolution of military technology during World War II.

Military-Industrial Complex

The military-industrial complex refers to the relationship between governments, military forces, and industries that produce weapons and technology. During World War II, countries relied heavily on large-scale production of weapons and equipment. World at War highlights this through its portrayal of mass-produced firearms and coordinated military efforts. This concept was discussed in lectures as a key factor in modern warfare, showing how economic and industrial systems are closely tied to military power.

Ethics of Technology

The ethics of technology examines whether technological advancements should be used simply because they are possible. Weapons such as flamethrowers and explosives raise serious ethical concerns due to their destructive nature. In World at War, these weapons are presented as tools for gameplay, but in reality, they caused immense suffering. This reflects class discussions about how technological innovation can create moral dilemmas, especially when used in warfare.

Connection to Course Material

These concepts were explored throughout the course in lectures and readings that examined how technology influences society. The game provides a modern way to engage with these ideas by simulating historical events and technologies. By connecting the gameplay experience to course concepts, it becomes clear that technology not only shapes warfare but also affects how society understands and represents it.
